How alerts work

The exact flow that decides when Ground Control buzzes, repeats, calls — and what turns it all off.
What starts an alert

1 A session reaches a stopping point

Claude finishes its turn (Stop hook) or pauses to ask you something (Notification hook). This arms a pending alert — it hasn't fired yet.

↓

2 Wait for 5 seconds of quiet debounce

Claude often pauses a couple seconds then keeps going on its own. So we wait: the session must sit continuously idle for 5s. If anything starts again — the main turn, a background sub‑agent, a shell — the 5‑second clock restarts. No more "alert, but it's still running."

↓
Gates it must pass to fire

3 You haven't already replied

If you sent a new message since it stopped, the stale alert is dropped (you're clearly on it).

4 It's a session you're waiting on

Only sessions you've sent to can alert — so random autonomous sessions stay silent. Override with Always alert in settings to buzz for every session.

5 Not muted

If you've silenced alerts for a while, nothing fires.

↓
What you get

6 🔔 The alert fires

A push on your phone and a native notification on your Mac. The session is marked unread.

7 🔁 Repeats

While it's still unread, you get re‑nudged on your chosen interval (e.g. every 30s).

8 📞 A phone call

If it stays unacknowledged for your Call me delay, your phone actually rings with a spoken message.

What turns it all off

✓ Only an active acknowledgment

Repeats and the call stop — and the badge clears — the moment you do one of these:

  • Open the session (tap into it)
  • Reply to it (send a message)
  • Tap the alert (which opens it)

Just having the session on screen does not count — a screen being open doesn't mean you saw it. It keeps nudging until you actually act.
